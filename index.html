Founder Fireside + Networking Event

This event brings together two founders with complementary experiences in scaling startups, offering students a unique opportunity to learn how early decisions and strategic choices compound over time.

Executive Summary

Targeted at 50–70 high-potential students interested in tech, biotech, and aspiring entrepreneurs, the session will include a moderated fireside chat, pre-screened audience Q&A, and structured networking.

50–70 Students 2 Hours Structured Networking

Purpose & Goals

For Morrissette

Approve a high-impact, low-risk event that enhances the Institute's programming, promotes founder-student engagement, and strengthens Western's entrepreneurial ecosystem.

For Founders

Connect with motivated students, share scaling insights, and identify potential collaborators, all with minimal time commitment.

For Students

Gain actionable lessons from real-world startup experiences, build strategic connections, and experience structured networking.

For Program & Community

Establish a repeatable, scalable model for future sessions that maximizes impact while respecting founders' time.

Proposed Founders / Speakers

Kooshiar Azimian

AI & Biotech Startup Founder

Offers a deep technical perspective from AI and biotech startups, emphasizing how early product, team, and architectural decisions influence long-term outcomes.

AI Biotech Technical Decisions

Martin Basiri

Global Scaling Founder

Brings firsthand experience scaling a Canadian company into a global organization, including fundraising, hiring at scale, and operational tradeoffs.

Global Scaling Fundraising Operations

Together, they provide students with an end-to-end view of scaling—from technical and product decisions to strategic and operational challenges.
